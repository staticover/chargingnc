Our Story

Built on Passion,
Driven by Innovation

For over three decades, we've been at the forefront of electric vehicle technology. What started as a passion project in the 1990s has evolved into North Carolina's most trusted EV infrastructure partner.

Our Journey

ChargingNC was born from a deep passion for electric vehicles that dates back to the 1990s. While most of the world was still skeptical about EVs, our founders were already building, tinkering, and having fun with electric cars. We saw the future before it arrived.

Over the past 30+ years, we've witnessed the evolution of EV technology firsthand—from experimental conversions to today's sophisticated electric vehicles. This isn't just a business opportunity for us; it's a calling we've answered for decades.

Our team has spent years providing technical guidance to the communications industry, building expertise in scalable infrastructure solutions. Now, with North Carolina's rapid EV growth, we've channeled that experience into supporting critical charging infrastructure, helping businesses and property owners stay ahead in the evolving transportation landscape.

We're not newcomers jumping on a trend. We're longtime advocates who've been championing electric transportation since before it was mainstream. Our passion has only grown stronger as the technology has matured and the infrastructure gap has become more apparent.

Who We Are

Expert Team, Local Focus

Education & Expertise

We educate our partners about every aspect of EV charging infrastructure. Understanding charger types, power requirements, and installation costs empowers stakeholders to make informed decisions that meet both current and future EV charging demands.

Staying informed about incentives, maintenance strategies, and revenue opportunities ensures long-term profitability and sustainability in the rapidly expanding EV market.

Data-Driven Approach

We help you define key performance indicators (KPIs) for EV charging infrastructure, ensuring efficiency and profitability. These include utilization rate (charger usage frequency), revenue per charger (income from sessions and subscriptions), and charger uptime (maintaining high availability and reliability).
